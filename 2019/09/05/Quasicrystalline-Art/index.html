Quasicrystalline Art

September 5, 2019

Quasicrystals, a beautiful manifestation of something without a strict crystalline symmetry but nonetheless shows order, have won a Nobel prize and have recently interested my own work with a dodecagonal graphene quasicrystal making its way into ScienceS. J. Ahn et al., Science 361, 782 (2018) .

This led to this beautiful cover in Science

We have been studying how quasiperiodicity interplays with materials that have Dirac nodes, including twisted bilayer graphene. While we have not studied graphene at 30-degrees like the work in Science, that is an extreme where all crystalline periodicity is lost. This phenomena is a perfect example of the kind of research I’ve been doing a lot with these days, and so it inspired the new logo for this website

One can tell how this is done: You find the points where two hexagons are on top of each other, put down a point, and connect. There are three shapes: a rhombus, an equilateral triangle, and a square. This can be done along the entire sheet to create an amazing looking pattern.
